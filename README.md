# Sugarcane-RedRot-Severity-Grading
Automated severity grading pipeline for Sugarcane Red Rot disease using CIELAB color space and Gabor filtering.

Grade	Leaf Symptoms	Severity Level
0 (Healthy)	No lesions; leaves remain green and turgid	No infection
1 (Mild)	Small, isolated red streaks or spots on midrib with faint margins	Low severity
2 (Moderate)	Clear red lesions with dark margins on midrib; some leaf drooping	Moderate severity
3 (Severe)	Extensive lesions spreading along midrib and blade; leaf yellowing and drying	High severity
4 (Very Severe)	Complete leaf blight, drooping, and necrosis; plant shows systemic decline	Very high severity


**Redrot review 1999-R.Viswanathan**

Early stage: Leaves may look slightly dull or lose their natural shine. The plant’s growth slows down because the fungus blocks nutrient flow inside the stalk.
Moderate infection: The leaf tips and margins start to dry out and turn yellowish or reddish-brown. This happens because the fungus damages the vascular system that carries water and nutrients.
Severe infection: Leaves become completely dry and brittle, often showing reddish streaks or patches. The whole plant may wilt, and the top leaves die first.
Characteristic symptom: A reddish discoloration sometimes appears along the midrib or veins, which helps farmers identify infected plants early.
